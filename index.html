How to Get to Mombasa

Mombasa is an important junction for all forms of transportation, so you can easily travel to Mombasa via, air, bus and railway. - By air: The nearest Mombasa Airport is Moi International Airport Mombasa, which is approximately 13 km away from the Mombasa city. Mombasa Airport is connected to rest if the country and other European cities by frequent international flights. There are Daily flights from Nairobi to Mombasa, the flights fly from Jomo Kenyatta International Airport and Wilson Airport. The flight to Mombasa from Nairobi takes an hour. The Mombasa flight prices varies depending on the airline that you are using, Airlines offering any flight to Mombasa are based Nairobi Kenyatta Airport and Wilson Airport. Airlines that fly to Mombasa from Nairobi are: Kenya Airways – Jambojet - Fly 540 - Skyward express - Silverstone Air - Mombasa Air Safaris Kenya Airways, Jambo jet and Fly 540 depart from Nairobi Airport JKIA, while the other three depart from Wilson Airport. All Flights to Mombasa to Nairobi take an hour at most and flights Prices vary depending on the season and the day of departure. The cost of a Mombasa air ticket versus the cost of a Mombasa train ticket is probably the biggest factor most travelers consider when choosing between flying to Mombasa or using the SGR train to Mombasa, the train is obviously much more affordable. A one-way train ticket from Nairobi to Mombasa goes for KES 2,000 on economy class and KES 4000 on first class, on the other hand, the price of air tickets to Mombasa varies wildly depending on many factors such as demand, Competition among several local Mombasa airlines has helped bring down the cost of Mombasa Flight tickets but despite the free market’s best efforts, the flight prices are still higher than that of train tickets. Mombasa is approximately 480 kilometers from Nairobi. Covering such a distance can be a time-consuming endeavor. A flight from Nairobi to Mombasa takes about 1hour and 15 more minutes if one is flying direct to Diani. The train, on the other hand, takes six hours. Furthermore, if one is traveling to Diani one has to then use a taxi service from the train station to Diani which is about 40 kilometers away. Further still, there is bound to be traffic in Mombasa, especially at the ferry crossing to get to the Mombasa, this can add a couple of hours to one’s journey from Nairobi. Such logistics leave Kenyans weighing between paying for an expensive flight to Mombasa and saving time or paying for an affordable train ticket to Mombasa but losing time that could be spent relaxing at Mombasa Beach hotel or by the Mombasa beach. A soft advantage, so to speak, of using the train is that it passes through Tsavo National Park where passengers are treated to the sight of wildlife such as elephants. As such, the experience of either flying to Mombasa or using the train is perhaps a bucket list item for many-a-tourist, and therefore, cost and time do not even come into consideration. Flying to Mombasa for the first time can be the highlight of one’s trip to Mombasa. Some travelers may save up and buy an expensive air ticket to Mombasa just to fulfill their dream of flying. On the other hand, using the train has a road trip feel to it. All one has to do is ensure one’s traveling party sits together, brings snacks, and buys some drinks, which are sold on the train by the way, and travel sharing stories and enjoying the scenery. On this point, it comes down to personal preference By Road: Mombasa Buses are not usually the favorite mode of transport in the city of Mombasa but they are available for travelling outside the city of Mombasa. When you reach the place, you can take a local taxi, tuk tuks or local busses to travel inside the city. The distance from Nairobi to Mombasa is approximately 480 kilometers. The drive from Nairobi to Mombasa will take you approximately 9 hours - If not driving to Mombasa you can hire a safari minivan or 4 x 4 land cruiser. There are also buses from Nairobi to Mombasa.

What to Do in Mombasa Kenya

What to Do in Mombasa – Mombasa Travel Guide - Mombasa is an Island on the Indian ocean and the capital of the wider Mombasa County that consists of several other nearby smaller islands. Mombasa is Kenya’s second largest metropolitan area after Nairobi and is located along the eastern coast of Kenya. Mombasa was at first referred to as “Mombassa”– meaning an Island of war. This was because the island was the center stage for countless wars of occupation by the early settlers (Arabs, Portuguese, Locals and the British).


Mombasa Fort Jesus

Mombasa Fort Jesus is the main tourist attraction in Mombasa, Kenya. The site is a 10th-century structure that was built by the Omani Arabs. Mombasa fort is not just a tourist attraction for locals and tourists to visit, but it also houses a mosque and houses many Islamic artifacts from around the world. In addition, it is an important symbol of the city as it represents its rich history as an Arab trading port. Mombasa Fort Jesus was originally built as Mombasa’s first fortress by the Omani Arabs in 1093 to guard against attacks from neighboring African tribes who were expanding their territory at the time. It mainly served as a strategic point because trade ships would anchor there before continuing into India or Arabia. In 1795, Fort Jesus was taken by British East India Company forces under Captain Robert.

Is Tap Water in Mombasa

Tap water is generally unsafe in Mombasa. You don’t want to drink the water (or use unpurified ice cubes on drinks) as it may contain bacteria. Mombasa gets its water from Mzima Springs, a natural spring in Tsavo West National Park. This water is treated and safe to drink. But the problem is the pipeline system – besides being compromised, these pipes can also have cracks that allow things to get into the water.
